Teaching & Training

In 2009, I began teaching basic bicycle mechanics classes to both youth and adults in an anti-oppression, worker co-operative framework with Free Ride. That experience, combined with my years of communications experience as a film producer and director, and my background in Theater of the Oppressed, awakened in me a deep passion for accessible community-based education.

Through offering dynamic and enriching trainings on a variety of subjects related to equitable and sustainable organizing, I hope to empower people and communities to better achieve their common aspirations. I offer engaging, out-of-the-box workshops on topics including co-operatives, Consensus decision-making, anti-oppression organizing, even sustainability in organizing.  I have taught in a broad range of communities and settings.

My Philosophy

“There must exist a paradigm, a practical model for social change that includes an understanding of ways to transform consciousness that are linked to efforts to transform structures.” ~ bell hooks

I believe accessible education and information is crucial to developing community resilience to oppressive structures.  As Paolo Freire wrote, "people's education" begins with awakening people to themselves as Subjects, not Objects, in a historical socio-economic and cultural moment--able to act in ways that change their situations.  After learning emerges practice, and through practice comes the physical building of alternative systems which further buffers communities from being targets of exploitation.  

Education is at the heart of what I do, because without education, one cannot envision, design, and act from a consciousness of alternatives.  Even the most conventionally "well-educated" person among us may only be aware of their dominant paradigm's models for how resources flow and how society works.  Popular (peer-to-peer) education emphasizes the right of any person, as an equal, to receive information and self-determine how they will utilize it in their lives and communities.
