Becoming Sustainable (A Meta-mentary) (Sept 2014)

 

Summary

In 2008, student filmmaker Caroline Savery set out to minimize her environmental footprint to a 'sustainable' level while living in urban Pittsburgh, PA, and documenting the process for a web-based video series called Sust Enable. What at first seems like a straightforward goal—to reduce her environmental footprint to within 'one seven-billionth of the Earth'--transforms into a deep inquiry about our Western society’s struggles to make sense of 'sustainability'. In Caroline's five-year journey to embody the true meaning of 'sustainability' in her life, we see reflections of a wide range of Americans grappling with the dawning awareness, devastation, and redemption in "living out of balance". As Caroline's awareness evolves, so too do the film's narrative and styles (coining the term 'meta-mentary' to describe the film's braiding of story themes and stylistic elements). Becoming Sustainable is a transformative film experience, not to be missed by anyone who has tried to figure out how to make the world a better place.

The Sust Enable webisode series (2008-2009)

  • Episode 3: Transportation (March 2009)
  • Episode 2: Shelter (Nov 2008)
  • Episode 1: Introduction (Sept 2008)

In 2008, Caroline Savery, a 21-year old environmental activist living in Pittsburgh, PA, USA, launched Sust Enable, a three-month attempt to live, as defined by the online Ecological Footprint Calculator, a “100% sustainable” lifestyle in urban Pittsburgh. That quest, which called for living in a tent, collecting rainwater, dumpster diving for food, and more, would be depicted in a web-based video episode series, giving viewers an example of how to implement sustainability into their own lives. Note: of eight planned and produced episodes, only the first three were released due to directorial abandonment of the initial premise.
